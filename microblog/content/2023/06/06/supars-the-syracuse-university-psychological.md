> SUPARS [the Syracuse University Psychological Abstracts Retrieval Service] and other largely forgotten systems were the forerunners of the contemporary search engines we have today. While the popular history of the internet valorises Silicon Valley coders – or, sometimes, the former US vice president Al Gore – many of the original concepts for search emerged from library scientists focused on the accessibility of documents in time and space.

[Ingenious librarians: A group of 1970s campus librarians foresaw our world of distributed knowledge and research, and designed search tools for it](https://aeon.co/essays/the-1970s-librarians-who-revolutionised-the-challenge-of-search)
