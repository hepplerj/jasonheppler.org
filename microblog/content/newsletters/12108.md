<p> </p> 
<p>Over the past few years I’ve been making changes to my technological life. It began a couple of years ago as I started to de-Google myself. Here’s where things stand at the moment:</p> <ul> <li>Twitter has been deactivated for almost <a href="https://jasonheppler.org/2022/11/19/0-tweets/">two years to the day</a>. I spend some time on BlueSky, but I’m <a href="https://social.jasonheppler.org/2024/11/08/becoming-newsresilient.html">pretty strict</a> about limiting myself there.</li> <li>I spend most of my social media-esque time at <a href="https://micro.blog/">Micro.blog</a> for both short and longer-form posts. I’ve also started a second Micro.blog called <a href="https://ink.jasonheppler.org">Tack &amp; Ink</a> designed to write about and share my current research project.</li> <li> <a href="https://buttondown.email/jheppler">My newsletter</a> has fizzled out, but I’m thinking about ways I might come back to it. In the meantime, you could subscribe to the <a href="https://social.jasonheppler.org/subscribe/">Micro.blog newsletters</a>.</li> <li>As part of making myself <a href="https://social.jasonheppler.org/2024/11/08/becoming-newsresilient.html">more news-resilient</a>, I’ve shifted most of my news reading to print publications: <em>The Atlantic</em>, <em>The Economist</em>, and <em>High Country News</em> are the primary sources, with the <em>Washington Post</em> and <em>New York Times</em> here and there. <em>The Economist</em>’s <a href="https://www.economist.com/the-world-in-brief">The World in Brief</a> is particularly good.</li> <li>I’m moving a lot of my stuff out of the cloud. Dropbox has become increasingly less reliable, so I’ve been slowly moving my things to a local hard drive (which I can access on my local network anytime, or sync down to other machines using Syncthing). A few things are stored in Apple iCloud, but it’s not a service I use much for file syncing.</li> <li>On services that have become unreliable, I’m also leaving 1Password for Apple Passwords. I don’t quite understand what happened to 1Password, but its reliability, especially its integration in Safari, is incredibly inconsistent.</li> <li>I mentioned above my attempts to de-Google myself. A few years ago I migrated all of my email to Fastmail, which I’m very happy with. I also moved all of my calendars to Apple iCloud. This is also undergoing some change: after years as a Fantastical user, I’m going back to the stock Apple Calendar. It does everything I need and, while it has a little more friction than Fantastical, it works well. I pair Calendar with <a href="https://www.mowglii.com/itsycal/">Itsycal</a> for menu bar access.</li> <li>DuckDuckGo is my default search engine (again, the great Google decoupling).</li> <li>I’m still <a href="https://jasonheppler.org/2024/07/15/how-i-use-obsidian/">solidly behind Obsidian</a> for note-taking, but having a fresh look at . . . notecards.</li> </ul>
