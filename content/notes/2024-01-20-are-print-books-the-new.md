I’ve started pondering to what degree we’ll return to printed books, bookstores, and libraries in the face of inane and inaccurate AI-generated material flooding Amazon and the Internet. 

I don’t find it an entirely bad thing that we do, but I’m also [in the business](https://rrchnm.org) of creating professional historical scholarship, educational resources, and public history on the web. I don’t know yet, it’s just a thought occurring lately and how I (and we, as a profession) might respond to this.

Another option is that we finally begin to settle on how we verify and peer review online scholarship. It’s a topic I’ve [mused on for a long time](/2012/02/05/journals-in-the-digital-age/), and we still haven’t found a great solution to. Perhaps this will light some urgency to the question.
